The policy seam is part of the owned core. Every tool call, artifact promotion, memory write, and approval flows through it, and every decision becomes a `policyDecision` audit record.

## Default-deny seam

The default policy engine is default-deny. Replacement engines must:

- preserve the `policyDecision` contract;
- still emit audit events for denials and approvals;
- still respect scopes, risk labels, and execution limits declared in capability manifests.

## What policy controls

- tool registry visibility and invocation
- approval modes and approver scope
- memory write/read scopes and retention windows
- artifact promotion gates
- sandbox filesystem and network policy
- secret resolution boundaries (`secretRef`)

## Failing closed

Current foundation fixtures cover fail-closed policy/runtime/evidence cases. See `pnpm policy:test` for the current gates.
