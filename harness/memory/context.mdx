Context assembly is the bridge between memory and the provider. Every turn receives a `contextPack` that has been:

- assembled from project, task, and artifact memory;
- filtered by the policy seam against the actor's scopes;
- redacted according to the redaction policy;
- bound to the run id, task id, and turn id;
- tagged with freshness and source attribution metadata.

## Why a contract here

A typed `contextPack` keeps the provider boundary thin. Providers receive only what policy allows, and every context decision shows up in the audit trail.
